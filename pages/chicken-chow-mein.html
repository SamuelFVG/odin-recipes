Chicken chow mein

Description

Upgrade your Chinese takeaway experience with this low-maintenance slow cooker solution! Traditionally stir-fried dishes like Chicken chow mein can now be enjoyed with minimal effort. This recipe prioritizes convenience, cooking the chicken first then adding vegetables and noodles near the end for a cohesive finish. Simply combine the ingredients with a flavorful sauce, set your slow cooker to the appropriate setting, and let it handle the rest. This method eliminates the need for high-heat cooking and constant pan attention, making it ideal for busy schedules or simply relaxed mealtimes. The result? Tender chicken, crisp vegetables, and perfectly cooked noodles bathed in a delicious sauce, all without the unnecessary mess or fuss. Enjoy a restaurant-quality experience from the comfort of your own kitchen with this efficient and effortless approach to a beloved Chinese classic.

Ingredients

  • 250ml (1 cup) Massel Chicken Style Liquid Stock
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Coles Asia Oyster Sauce
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tsp finely grated ginger
  • 1 brown onion, cut into thin wedges
  • 1 large carrot, peeled, halved lengthways, sliced
  • 2 chicken marylands, skin removed
  • 3 tsp cornflour
  • 250g dried egg noodles
  • 100g snow peas, halved diagonally
  • 410g can baby corn spears, drained, halved
  • Chopped fresh coriander leaves, to serve

Steps

  1. Combine stock, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil and ginger in a slow cooker. Add the onion, carrot and chicken. Cover and cook for 5 hours on LOW, turning chicken halfway through cooking, if possible.
  2. Remove the chicken from the slow cooker and place on a chopping board. Turn the slow cooker to HIGH. Combine cornflour with 1 tablespoon water, stirring until smooth. Stir into the liquid in slow cooker. Cover and cook for 15 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, cook noodles in a large saucepan of boiling water for 3 minutes, adding the snow peas for the last 1 minute of cooking time. Drain and run under cold water. Set aside. Use a fork to shred chicken from the bone.
  4. Add the chicken, noodles, snow peas and corn to the slow cooker. Use tongs to toss to combine.
  5. Sprinkle the chow mein with coriander to serve.
